The conflict takes place in 2025. In that year, the United States is led by Democratic President Linwood Sutter, and Russia is led by President Victor Grishenko, who succeeded Vladimir Putin following his death from a heart attack in 2018. President Grishenko demands that the United States recognize Russia’s annexation of the Svalbard Archipelago in the Arctic Ocean.

Background:

In the years prior to the conflict, Russia made two claims over Arctic territory:

  • First, Russia petitioned the UN Commission on the Limits of the Continental Shelf (CLCS) to expand its territorial possessions in the Arctic region. In its petition, Russia claimed a vast 460,000 sq. miles region of seafloor connected to the Lomonosov Ridge as the natural extension of its continental shelf. This territorial claim overlapped with claims made by Canada, Denmark, Norway, and the United States.
  • Second, Russia filed a separate complaint at the International Court of Justice (ICJ) about Norway’s behavior in the Svalbard Archipelago, an Arctic island granted to Norway by the 1920 Svalbard Treaty that lies beyond Russia’s territorial claims. Norway had begun drilling for offshore oil around Svalbard, claiming that the surrounding waters are part of Norwegian national territory. However, Russia argued that Norway was in violation of the Svalbard Treaty, which guarantees equal access to Svalbard’s natural resources for all signatories, and demanded that the ICJ reaffirm Russia’s rights.
